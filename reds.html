Beaujolais Villages AOP

La Cascade

"La Cascade" is an expression of the Gamay variety. It is crafted from a traditional Beaujolais semi carbonic maceration. It has a pleasant bouquet of ripe, red fruit as well as stone fruit. It is structured, but very drinkable.

  • Variety: Gamay
  • Aging: 100% tank
  • Production: 3,000 - 5,000 bottles
  • Storage: 2 - 6 years

Beaujolais Villages AOP

La Tour

This wine is the result of long and delicate maceration with very ripe, partialy destemmed grapes followed with long aging in wood. “La Tour” has the flavors of very ripe red and dark berries and may be kept for several years. There are also hints of licorice. This vintage is atypical and bold.

  • Variety: Gamay
  • Aging: 60% tank, 40% barrel (10-12 months)
  • Production: 2,000 - 4,000 bottles
  • Storage: 3 - 8 years

Fleurie AOP

Clos de la Madone (Monopole)

Our Fleurie is made to express as best as possible the richness and complexity of La Madone, a hill at the bottom of wich shines the Clos la Madone. We are the only owners of the Clos. It is therefore a monopoly. Maceration and aging are long to express the complexity of the terroir. This great wine emanates aromas of red and black berries, spices with pepper but also liquorice and cocoa.

  • Variety: Gamay
  • Aging: 50% tank, 50% barrel (18-22 months)
  • Production: 2,000 - 4,000 bottles
  • Storage: 3 - 10 years

Comtés Rhodaniens IGP

Syrah

Implementing Syrah in Beaujolais more than 10 years ago was a real anticipation of global warming. The wine produced expresses the grape variety fully when it is grown to northern regions. There are aromas of black berries (blackcurrant, blueberry) and spices are also very present. These are punctuated by floral notes (violet) and others grilled notes. The long macerations and associated aging times make it a powerful wine with silky tannins.

  • Variety: Syrah
  • Aging: 50% tank, 50% barrel (18-22 months)
  • Production: 1,500 - 3,000 bottles
  • Storage: 3 - 10 years
